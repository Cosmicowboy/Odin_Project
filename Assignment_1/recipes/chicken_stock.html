Chicken Stock

Skip pre amble, take me to the recipe

Why Make This

  • You get three adaptable ways to make chicken stock using either leftover bones or raw parts.
  • Homemade stock is full of iron, collagen, and vitamins you won't get from boxed broths.
  • You can simmer it for hours, sauté bones for rich flavor, or whip up a quick batch in just one hour.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound bone-in chicken parts
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 3 stalks celery ribs with leaves, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 large carrot, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • ½ teaspoons salt
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 6 cups water
  • ¼ cup cold water (Optional)
  • 1 large egg (Optional)

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Ingredients to make homemade chicken stock
  2. Place chicken parts, onion, celery, carrot, salt, and cloves in a large soup pot or Dutch oven. A large pot with pieces of chicken, onion, carrots, celery, salt, and cloves
  3. Add 6 cups water. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 1 hour. A large pot of simmering water with chicken, seasonings, and vegetables
  4. Remove chicken and vegetables. A large pot of stock next to a bowl of skimmed chicken pieces and vegetables
  5. Strain stock. A mesh sieve over a bowl of chicken stock
  6. Skim fat off the surface. A hand holding a spoon, skimming fat off the surface of a bowl of chicken stock
  7. To clarify stock for clear soup, removing solid flecks that are too small to be strained out with cheesecloth, follow this method: Separate egg white from egg yolk and reserve the shell. A hand holding an open egg shell with a yolk, over a bowl of egg whites
  8. Combine 1/4 cup cold water, egg white, and crushed eggshell in a small bowl. A small bowl of crushed egg shell, water, and egg whites
  9. Add to strained stock and bring to a boil. Remove from heat and let stand for 5 minutes. A pot of simmering, foamy chicken stock
  10. Strain again through a sieve lined with cheesecloth.
